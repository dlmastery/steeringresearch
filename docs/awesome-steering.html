01Foundations — the activation-addition lineage

The train-free, linear, residual-stream-addition family: compute a direction (difference-of-means or PCA on contrastive pairs) and add / subtract / project it at inference time, no weight updates.

  • Inference-Time Intervention: Eliciting Truthful Answers from a Language Model (ITI) — Li, Patel, Viégas, Pfister, Wattenberg, 2023 NeurIPS spotlight. arXiv:2306.03341. Shifts activations along truthful-vs-false directions on a few attention heads (diff-of-means on heads, found by linear probing). Key result: truthfulness on TruthfulQA 32.5% → 65.1% on Alpaca.
  • Steering Language Models With Activation Engineering (ActAdd) — Turner, Thiergart, Leech, Udell, Vazquez, Mini, MacDiarmid, 2023 (rev. 2024). arXiv:2308.10248. Contrasts activations on prompt pairs (“Love” − “Hate”) to compute a vector added in the forward pass. Key result: SOTA sentiment shift + detoxification with single-pair efficiency, off-target capabilities preserved.
  • Representation Engineering: A Top-Down Approach to AI Transparency (RepE) — Zou, Phan, Chen, Campbell, … Hendrycks, 2023. arXiv:2310.01405. Population-level representations (not neurons) at the center; contrastive reading vectors monitor and manipulate concepts. Key result: >90% accuracy distinguishing harmful/harmless even under jailbreaks; emotion steering raises harmful compliance despite RLHF.
  • Steering Llama 2 via Contrastive Activation Addition (CAA) — Panickssery, Gabrieli, Schulz, Tong, Hubinger, Turner, 2024 ACL. arXiv:2312.06681. Averages residual-stream activation differences between positive/negative example pairs; adds at all post-prompt positions with a signed coefficient. Key result: significantly alters sycophancy / hallucination / refusal on Llama-2, stacks over finetuning + system prompts, minimal capability loss.
  • Refusal in Language Models Is Mediated by a Single Direction — Arditi, Obeso, Syed, Paleka, Panickssery, Gurnee, Nanda, 2024 NeurIPS. arXiv:2406.11717. Diff-of-means of harmful vs harmless instruction activations yields one refusal direction; ablate to bypass, add to induce. Key result: erasing prevents refusal, adding elicits refusal on harmless prompts, across 13 models up to 72B.
  • Activation Interventions Can Be Transferred Between Large Language Models — Oozeer, Nathawani, Prakash, Lan, Harrasse, Abdullah, 2025 ICML poster. arXiv:2503.04429. Transfers pre-computed diff-of-means vectors across distinct LLMs for the same safety behaviors. Key result: predictable transfer for backdoor removal and harmful-prompt refusal.
  • Scaling Laws for Activation Steering with Llama 2 Models and Refusal Mechanisms — Ali et al., 2025. arXiv:2507.11771. A CAA scaling study across Llama-2 sizes. Key result: effectiveness grows with model size but saturates differently for refusal. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • StyleVector: Personalized Text Generation with Contrastive Activation Steering — J. Zhang, Wang, Chua et al., 2025 ACL long. UNVERIFIED — ACL Anthology confirmed, no standalone arXiv ID; search “StyleVector contrastive activation” Derives a user-specific style vector by contrasting authentic-user vs generic-model activations, added for personalization. Key result: matches/exceeds RAG and PEFT on stylistic consistency at negligible cost. NEEDS VERIFICATION

02Conditional / gated steering (the meta-layer)

Not peer injectors — a meta-layer that reads context and decides whether to apply a behavior vector. The gate is a read-only probe on a different axis/layer than the write, so it stacks on top of nearly every method in §1.

  • Programming Refusal with Conditional Activation Steering (CAST) — B. W. Lee, Padhi, Ramamurthy, Miehling, Dognin, Nagireddy, Dhurandhar, 2025 ICLR Spotlight. arXiv:2409.05907. Condition vector (PCA on contrastive prompt activations) + thresholding gates a behavior vector; OR/AND/complement composition. Key result: “if hate speech then refuse” keeps harmful-refusal high, harmless-refusal ≈2% (Qwen-1.8B). NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • FineSteer: A Unified Framework for Fine-Grained Inference-Time Steering — Weng, J. Zhang, Cai, Y. Li, P. Wang, Tian, 2026. arXiv:2604.15488. Subspace-guided Conditional Steering (SCS) energy-ratio gate, then Mixture-of-Steering-Experts (MoSE). Key result: +7.6% TruthfulQA over Llama-3, near-100% safety, minimal utility loss; SCS is a direct CAST successor. Gemma-2-9B-IT NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Selective Steering: Norm-Preserving Control Through Discriminative Layer Selection — Quy-Anh Dang, Chris Ngo, 2026 ACL. arXiv:2601.19375. Norm-preserving rotation applied only at discriminative layers (µpos·µneg≤0) — a layer-gate. Key result: 5.5× higher ASR, zero perplexity violations, ≈100% retention; 82.69% ASR on gemma-2-2b where SAS=0%. Gemma-2-2B/9B-IT NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • In-Distribution Steering: Balancing Control and Coherence (IDS) — IDS team (CAA/MERA comparisons), 2025. arXiv:2510.13285. Input-dependent modulation keeps steered activations in-distribution before addition. Key result: avg rank 1.67 over 6 behaviors vs CAA-1's 3.07; refusal SPI 0.92 on Gemma-2-2B at PPL<8. Gemma-2-2B/9B-IT NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Contextual Linear Activation Steering of Language Models — Brandon Hsu et al., 2026. arXiv:2604.24693. Extends linear diff-of-means with input-dependent contextual modulation (projection conditioned on prompt embedding). Key result: stable multi-turn coherence over baseline CAA while preserving >98% MMLU. NEEDS VERIFICATION

03Learned / flow / distilled steering

Methods that learn the intervention — a low-rank map, a token-specific gain field, a velocity field, or a hypernetwork — rather than reading a fixed difference-of-means vector.

  • ReFT / LoReFT: Representation Finetuning for Language Models — Wu, Arora, Z. Wang, Geiger, Jurafsky, Manning, Potts, 2024 NeurIPS. arXiv:2404.03592. Learns low-rank interventions on frozen-model representations (a learned subspace edit) instead of weight updates. Key result: 15×–65× more parameter-efficient than prior PEFT, competitive/superior on commonsense + reasoning.
  • Improved Representation Steering for Language Models (RePS / BiPO objective) — Wu et al., 2025 NeurIPS poster. arXiv:2505.20809. Bidirectional preference objective (steer + suppress) trains low-rank reference-free preference steering. Key result: beats LM-objective training, narrows gap to prompting on Gemma 2B–27B, jailbreak-resilient. Gemma-2-2B/9B, Gemma-3-12B/27B NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • HyperSteer: Hypernetwork-Generated Steering Vectors — HyperSteer team, 2025. arXiv:2506.03292. A hypernetwork generates a steering vector conditioned on a natural-language prompt, replacing per-behavior extraction. Key result: prompt-conditioned vectors generalize across behaviors without re-extraction. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Steer Like the LLM: Activation Steering that Mimics Prompting (PSR) — Geert Heyman, Frederik Vandeputte, 2026 ICML. arXiv:2605.03907. Distills the token-specific gain g(ht) that real prompting induces — a learned per-token coefficient field on a fixed direction. Key result: outperforms existing steering, compares favorably to prompting on AxBench + persona (0.618 @ 2B-L20, 0.900 @ 9B-L20). Gemma-2-2B/9B-IT NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Beyond Steering Vector: Flow-based Activation Steering (FLAS) — Jin, Deng, J. Wang, Shen, C. Zhang, 2026. arXiv:2605.05892. Learns a concept-conditioned velocity field vt(h,t,c) transporting unsteered → steered activations (curved, multi-step) from a ~97.6M-param FlowBlock. Key result: first learned method to consistently beat prompting on AxBench held-out (HMean 1.015 @ 2B-IT, 1.113 @ 9B-IT), no per-concept tuning. Gemma-2-2B/9B-IT NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • YaPO: Learnable Sparse Activation Steering Vectors for Domain Adaptation — Abdine, Nakov, Vazirgiannis, Shang et al., 2026. arXiv:2601.08441. Reference-free policy optimization learns trainable sparse steering vectors in a pretrained SAE latent space (BiPO-style), added at inference. Key result: faster convergence, no measurable MMLU degradation, consistent gains over CAA on safety. Gemma-2-2B / 9B-It NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • LinEAS: End-to-end Learning of Activation Steering with a Distributional Loss — NeurIPS 2025 team. UNVERIFIED — poster confirmed, no arXiv ID; search “LinEAS activation steering” Trains linear maps with a global distributional loss across all layers plus sparsifying regularizers. Key result: beats comparable baselines on toxicity using unpaired data only, competitive with oracle methods. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Activation Steering for Aligned Open-ended Generation without Sacrificing Coherence (StTP / StMP) — StTP/StMP team, 2026. arXiv:2604.08169. Targeted residual addition with trait prompts (StTP) / multi-prompt (StMP) to restore honesty/compassion under malicious system prompts. Key result: recovers traits while preserving MMLU / MT-Bench / AlpacaEval and reducing repetition. NEEDS VERIFICATION

04Geometry-aware steering

The 2026 geometry wave: the residual stream is not flat Euclidean ℝd. These works replace find the right direction with find the right geometry — curved paths, spherical/hyperbolic/cylindrical metrics, gauge/identifiability, trajectory dynamics.

  • Angular Steering — Angular Steering team, 2025. arXiv:2510.26243. Rotates the activation within a 2D plane toward the target instead of adding (spherical-family anchor). Key result: directional control without norm increase — but a norm-violation flaw on <7B models that Selective Steering later fixes. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • GeoSteer: Faithful Chain-of-Thought Steering via Latent Manifold Gradients — Kazama et al., 2026. arXiv:2601.10229. Steers reasoning by following gradients along the latent manifold across CoT steps (trajectory-aware, axes 8+11). Key result: +0.9 accuracy / +4.5 reasoning-quality points avg vs baseline. NEEDS VERIFICATION / NEEDS REPLICATION
  • On the Non-Identifiability of Steering Vectors — Venkatesh & Kurapath (Manipal), 2026 ICLR workshop. arXiv:2602.06801. Theory: under white-box single-layer access, steering vectors are fundamentally non-identifiable — large behaviorally-indistinguishable equivalence classes exist (large readout null space). Key result: behavioral testing alone cannot recover “the” direction; structural constraints required (axis 10).
  • Curveball Steering: The Right Direction To Steer Isn't Always Linear — Raval, Song, Wu, Harrasse, Phillips, Barez, Abdullah, 2026. arXiv:2603.09313. The optimal steering path is curved; respecting manifold curvature beats a global linear direction (axis 8). Key result: geometry-aware nonlinear steering reduces off-manifold displacement at matched behavior change. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Not All Latent Spaces Are Flat: Hyperbolic Concept Control (HyCon) — Briglia, Facchiano, Cursi, Sampieri, Rodolà et al., 2026. arXiv:2603.14093. Models hierarchical/tree-like concepts in hyperbolic (negative-curvature) space, where they embed with low distortion (axis 9). Key result: increased stability (“geometric pacing under strong steering”) vs Euclidean. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Global Evolutionary Refined Steering (GER-steer): Cross-Layer Consistency — X. Jiang, W. Yu, D. Wang, L. Hu, 2026. arXiv:2603.12298. Training-free extraction of a global invariant direction across layers via spectral consensus + evolutionary refinement before addition. Key result: AdvBench refusal ↑ 0.948 on Gemma-2-9B-it (vs ~0.77), superior transfer across five domains. Gemma-2-9B-it NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • The Cylindrical Representation Hypothesis (CRH) — Gao, Zhang, Liu, … Chen (MBZUAI), 2026 ICML. arXiv:2605.01844. Representation differences live on a cylinder Sd-1×ℝ: radial (magnitude) × angular (semantic) components (axis 9). Key result: decomposing into radius (strength) × angle (meaning) makes steering more stable and predictable. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Manifold Steering — Wurgaft, Rager, Kowal, … Goodman, Fel, Geiger, Lubana (Goodfire/Stanford/Harvard), 2026. arXiv:2605.05115. Fits an activation manifold Mh and behavior manifold My; steering along Mh follows My, while linear steering cuts through off-manifold regions. Key result: “recasts steering from finding the right direction to finding the right geometry”; bidirectional link holds across reasoning geometries + a video world model. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Manifold-Guided Attention Steering (MAGS) — I. Li, Guruprasad, Sengupta, Satish, D'Antoni, R. Yu, 2026. arXiv:2605.21770. Learns a correctness manifold vs error subspace, then projects attention-head outputs back onto it before WO, writing the correction into the KV cache (axes 1+11). Key result: up to +10.8% on MATH-500/GSM8K/HumanEval/MBPP, beating ITI / Angular / Contrastive Decoding. corpus “Gemma-4” claim is fabricated
  • Steered LLM Activations are Non-Surjective — Mishra, Khashabi, A. Liu, 2026 (v2). arXiv:2604.09839. Frames addition as a surjectivity problem; proves steered states lie off the manifold reachable by any discrete prompt. Key result: “almost surely no prompt can reproduce” steered behavior; L2 distances confirm no close preimages. Gemma-3-1B NEEDS VERIFICATION

05Sparse / SAE-feature steering

Steering in the sparse, interpretable basis of a Sparse Autoencoder — clamp or add along monosemantic feature directions instead of dense residual directions. Anchored by GemmaScope.

  • Towards Monosemanticity: Decomposing Language Models With Dictionary Learning — Bricken, Templeton, … Olah (Anthropic), 2023 Transformer Circuits. transformer-circuits.pub/2023 (no arXiv). First large-scale demonstration that SAEs recover thousands of monosemantic, causally steerable features. Key result: 512-neuron MLP → >4,096 (up to 131k) features; feature pinning steers output.
  • Jumping Ahead: Improving Reconstruction Fidelity with JumpReLU SAEs — Rajamanoharan, Lieberum, Sonnerat, Conmy, Varma, Kramár, Nanda (DeepMind), 2024. arXiv:2407.14435. Learned-threshold JumpReLU + direct L0 penalty via straight-through estimators. Key result: SOTA reconstruction-sparsity Pareto on Gemma-2-9B (beats Gated & TopK). Gemma-2-9B
  • Scaling Monosemanticity: Interpretable Features from Claude 3 Sonnet (Golden Gate Claude) — Templeton, Conerly, … Henighan (Anthropic), 2024 Transformer Circuits. transformer-circuits.pub/2024 (no arXiv). Scales SAEs to production Claude 3 Sonnet; feature clamping as a steering primitive. Key result: ~34M features; the Golden Gate feature clamped to 10× makes the model obsess about the bridge while staying coherent.
  • Gemma Scope: Open Sparse Autoencoders Everywhere All At Once on Gemma 2 — Lieberum, Rajamanoharan, Conmy, Smith, Sonnerat, Varma, Kramár, Dragan, Shah, Nanda (DeepMind), 2024 BlackboxNLP. arXiv:2408.05147. Open release of JumpReLU SAEs covering every layer/sublayer of Gemma 2. Key result: >400 SAEs / >30M features — the infrastructure under nearly all small-Gemma sparse steering. Gemma-2-2B/9B
  • Improving Steering Vectors by Targeting Sparse Autoencoder Features (SAE-TS) — Chalnev, Siu, Conmy, 2024 (rev. 2025). arXiv:2411.02193. Uses SAEs to measure side-effects of any vector, then optimizes a replacement activating only the desired feature. Key result: better effect/coherence trade-off than CAA and single-feature steering. Gemma-2-2B (L12)
  • Interpretable Steering with Feature Guided Activation Additions (FGAA) — Soo, Guang, Teng, Balaganesh, Guoxian, Ming, 2025. arXiv:2501.09929. Combines CAA + SAE-TS in SAE latent space (16,384 features, Gemma Scope L12), optimizing feature selection. Key result: outperforms CAA, SAE-decoder, and SAE-TS; optimal in 8/9 tasks on 2B. Gemma-2-2B/9B
  • AxBench: Even Simple Baselines Outperform Sparse Autoencoders — Wu, Arora, Geiger, Z. Wang, Huang, Jurafsky, Manning, Potts (Stanford), 2025 ICML spotlight. arXiv:2501.17148. Apples-to-apples benchmark separating concept detection vs steering across prompting / finetuning / probes / steering vectors / ReFT / SAEs. Key result (verbatim): “On both evaluations, SAEs are not competitive”; prompting wins steering, probes win detection; ReFT-r1 is the competitive interpretable method. Gemma-2-2B/9B
  • Steering Large Language Model Activations in Sparse Spaces (SAS) — Bayat, Rahimi-Kalahroudi, Pezeshki, Chandar, Vincent, 2025. arXiv:2503.00177. Isolates behavior-specific SAE features via contrastive prompt-pairing to reinforce/suppress in sparse space. Key result: nuanced modulation at λ=±1, τ=0.7; scaling SAEs improves monosemanticity, beats dense baselines. Gemma-2-2B (main), 9B
  • Interpretable LLM Guardrails via Sparse Representation Steering (SRS) — He et al., 2025. arXiv:2503.16851. Disentangles activations via Gemma-Scope SAE; selects dimensions via bidirectional KL on positive/negative pairs. Key result: improved controllability across safety/fairness/truthfulness (single- and multi-attribute), quality preserved. Gemma-2-2B/9B-IT NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • CorrSteer: Generation-Time Steering via Correlated SAE Feature Selection — Cho, Z. Wu, Koshiyama, 2025 (v3 2026). arXiv:2508.12535. Correlates SAE-feature activations on generated tokens with labels on-the-fly (O(1) streaming, no contrast dataset), then steers with top-correlated features. Key result: +3.3% MMLU, +27.1% HarmBench on Gemma-2-2B with lower side-effect ratio than finetuning. Gemma-2-2B NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Steered Generation via Gradient-Based Optimization on Sparse Query Features — Bhattacharyya et al., 2026. arXiv:2605.23040. SAEs on query activations, decomposed into sparse features, gradient-optimized at inference to align with class prototypes. Key result: progressive refinement with cognitive-style accuracy gains over dense query steering (Bloom/Phi). NEEDS VERIFICATION

06Attention / KV / decoding-site steering

Interventions that leave the residual stream and act on the attention mechanism (Q/V vectors, attention scores), the KV cache, or the decoding distribution — orthogonal sites that stack with residual methods and often cost less.

  • DoLa: Decoding by Contrasting Layers Improves Factuality — Chuang et al., 2023 ICLR. arXiv:2309.03883. Contrasts logits from a mature vs premature layer at decode time (logit-site, after all residual edits). Key result: improves TruthfulQA + factual generation across LLaMA sizes, no retraining.
  • Tell Your Model Where to Attend: Post-hoc Attention Steering (PASTA) — Q. Zhang, Singh, L. Liu, X. Liu, B. Yu, Gao, T. Zhao, 2024 ICLR. arXiv:2311.02262. Profiles a few heads offline, then upweights user-marked tokens in those heads at inference. Key result: +22% avg accuracy on LLaMA-7B across instruction-following / knowledge-integration.
  • Spotlight Your Instructions: Dynamic Attention Steering (SpotLight) — Venkateswaran, Contractor, 2025 EACL long. arXiv:2505.12025. Monitors attention mass on instruction tokens each step, applies proportional only-when-needed logit bias to attention scores. Key result: fixes PASTA's offline-profiling cost and rigid targeting; strong on multi-turn / refusal / complex instructions.
  • KV Cache Steering for Controlling Frozen LLMs — Belitsky, Kopiczko, Dorkenwald, Mirza, Glass, Snoek, Asano, 2025 (v2). arXiv:2507.08799. Builds steering vectors from teacher traces, applies one additive edit to the KV cache after prompt processing (zero per-token overhead). Key result: +7.4% MATH, +4.6% GPQA-Diamond on Llama-3.1-70B; 95% style-transfer on small models. NEEDS VERIFICATION — no Gemma
  • DISCO: Disentangled Communication Steering — Torop, Masoomi, Eskandar, Dy, 2025 NeurIPS. arXiv:2509.16820. Injects steering vectors directly into the query and value spaces inside attention heads (not the residual stream). Key result: steering efficacy up to 19.1% higher than the runner-up. Gemma-2-9B NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Memory Inception: Latent-Space KV Cache Manipulation — A. Z. Liu, M. Zhang, Greenberg, Alnasser, Baker, Sous, 2026 (v2). arXiv:2605.06225. Training-free encoding of reminder text into frozen-model KV vectors injected at selected layers as hidden slots. Key result: 60–118× KV-storage reduction vs full prompting; beats CAA on control-drift and prompting on structured reasoning. NEEDS VERIFICATION — no Gemma
  • Periodic KV Cache Consolidation for Generalised Reasoning — Oomerjee et al., 2025 (v3). arXiv:2505.16950. A non-causal auxiliary Cache Processor rewrites KV entries at reasoning-step boundaries, consolidating recent + top-k prior entries. Key result: better long-horizon generalization; frames KV edits as a third pillar beside token-mediated and residual steering. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Don't Lose Focus: Activation Steering via Key-Orthogonal Projection (SKOP) — SKOP / Prompt-Activation Duality team, 2026. arXiv:2605.06342. Projects steering vectors orthogonal to the key matrix so additions don't contaminate the KV cache. Key result: retains >95% of vanilla query-space efficacy, utility degradation under 10%; best trade-off (rank 2/44) on Gemma-2-9B-IT. Gemma-2-9B-IT NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Improving Activation Steering via Attention-Level Delta Gating and Cropping (GCAD) — GCAD team, 2026. arXiv:2605.10664 UNVERIFIED — ID flagged in source Cropped, gated multi-layer attention-delta steering (instead of raw residual) to mitigate KV-cache contamination. Key result: higher per-trait expression Δ and coherence than single-layer residual baselines on Llama-3.1-8B / Qwen2.5-7B. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • AntiPaSTO: Self-Supervised Honesty Steering via Anti-Parallel Representations — M. J. Clark et al., 2026. arXiv:2601.07473. Learns antiparallel steering directions operating through attention and KV-cache pathways (beyond residual) for bidirectional control. Key result: 6.9× Steering F1 over prompting on Gemma-3-1B (DailyDilemmas); tables span Gemma-3-270M → 12B. Gemma-3-270M/1B/4B/12B NEEDS VERIFICATION

07Composition / stacking

Algebras and frameworks built to combine multiple steering goals without interference — the explicit answer to “can I run several safety vectors at once?”

  • Steering LLMs using Conceptors: Improving Addition-Based Activation Engineering — Postmus, Abreu, Müller et al., 2024. arXiv:2410.16314. Represents activation sets as ellipsoidal conceptor / steering-matrix regions, enabling AND/OR/NOT Boolean composition. Key result: the AND-combined conceptor outperformed mean-combined steering vectors on multi-goal tasks. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • From Steering Vectors to Conceptors: Compositional Affine Activation Steering — Abreu et al., 2025 OpenReview. UNVERIFIED — OpenReview, no arXiv ID in sources Conceptor matrices for affine composition of addition vectors. Key result: provably optimal compositional control with low utility loss on multi-property steering. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • EasyEdit2: An Easy-to-use Steering Framework for Editing LLMs — EasyEdit2 team, 2025. arXiv:2504.15133. Unified framework: CAA/STA + vector merging (Linear / TIES / DARE-TIES) for multi-task residual addition in one intervention. Key result: merged safety+sentiment CAA vectors reach joint scores (e.g. 66.89 safety on Gemma) vs single-task baselines. Gemma (L24), Qwen NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Persona Vectors: Monitoring and Controlling Character Traits in Language Models — Anthropic persona-vectors team, 2025. arXiv:2507.21509. Reusable directions for distinct traits (evil, sycophancy, hallucination) extracted automatically from trait descriptions — a bank of vectors per property. Key result: persona vectors predict and preventatively steer trait shifts (incl. during finetuning). NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Fine-Grained Activation Steering: Steering Less, Achieving More (AUSteer) — Feng, T. Li, Zhu, H. Zhou, Qian, L. Zhang, Chua, Mak, Ng, Mao, 2026. arXiv:2602.04428. Identifies discriminative atomic units via activation momenta and assigns adaptive per-unit strengths, steering only beneficial activations. Key result: +1.85 over SADI on LLaMA2-7B-Chat, +1.91 on Gemma2-9B-it, steering ≤100 activations vs thousands for CAA/STA. Gemma2-9B-it NEEDS VERIFICATION

08Safety & the Rogue Scalpel

The frontier finding that steering damages alignment as a side effect of leaving the manifold — plus the mechanistic and scaling work that frames it.

  • The Rogue Scalpel: Activation Steering Compromises LLM Safety — Korznikov, Galichin, Dontsov, Rogov, Oseledets, Tutubalina, 2025 (v2 2026) ICML. arXiv:2509.22067. On JailbreakBench (baseline compliance 0%), random/benign steering breaks refusal. Key results: random directions → 1–13% compliance; benign SAE features as bad or worse (817/1000 jailbreak ≥1 prompt); peaks in early-middle layers; averaging 20 weak vectors yields a universal attack (Falcon3-7B 5.7%→63.4%); App. E — attacks near-orthogonal to the refusal direction (cos 0.027±0.021), so the mechanism is off-manifold displacement, not refusal cancellation.
  • What Drives Representation Steering? A Mechanistic Case Study on Steering Refusal — S. Cheng, Wiegreffe, Manocha, 2026. arXiv:2604.08524. Circuit analysis of refusal steering: primary interaction via the OV circuit, largely ignoring QK; 90–99% sparsification. Key result: freezing all attention scores drops performance only 8.75%; vectors sparsify to 90–99% with most performance retained. Gemma-2-2B (L15) NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Scaling Laws for Activation Steering with Llama 2 Models and Refusal Mechanisms — Ali et al., 2025. arXiv:2507.11771. Explicit scaling laws for CAA-style refusal steering (cross-listed from §1). Key result: effectiveness grows with model size but saturates differently for refusal. NEEDS VERIFICATION
  • Refusal Steering: Fine-grained Control over LLM Refusal Behaviour for Sensitive Topics — García-Ferrero, Montero, Orus, 2025. arXiv:2512.16602. Replaces pattern-based refusal detection with an LLM-as-judge and a ridge-regularized refusal–compliance direction to remove political refusal while keeping harmful-content safety. Key result: maintains safety on JailbreakBench and near-baseline general performance. NEEDS VERIFICATION

09Evaluation & benchmarks

The measurement substrate. Steering has four things to measure at once — did behavior change, did capability survive, did text stay coherent, did safety survive — plus selectivity for gated methods.

  • AxBench: Even Simple Baselines Outperform Sparse Autoencoders — Wu, Arora, Geiger, Z. Wang, Huang, Jurafsky, Manning, Potts (Stanford), 2025 ICML spotlight. arXiv:2501.17148. The canonical fine-grained concept-steering benchmark + apples-to-apples harness; supplies the 500-concept holdout used by FLAS/PSR. Key result: “SAEs are not competitive” for steering; prompting wins. Gemma-2-2B/9B
  • JailbreakBench — the safety-integrity benchmark (100 harmful prompts, 10 categories), the Rogue Scalpel testbed; baseline compliance must be 0%, measure Compliance Rate. Mandatory for stacking/guard experiments.
  • HarmBench — broader harmful-behavior evaluation; cross-checks Compliance-Rate generalization beyond JailbreakBench.
  • AdvBench — harmful behaviors + strings; the refusal-rate target reported by GER-steer and Selective Steering.
  • StrongREJECT — stricter jailbreak scoring to avoid LLM-judge over-counting of “successful” jailbreaks.
  • XSTest — the over-refusal dual: benign prompts that look harmful, measuring the over-refusal cost of safety steering.
  • TruthfulQA — truthfulness (MC + generative); ITI's home benchmark and the FineSteer +7.6% target.
  • Capability & coherence probes — MMLU / ARC / GSM8K / HellaSwag (capability-tax, ≤1–2% drop = acceptable); WikiText-103 perplexity + repetition/MAUVE + LLM-judge coherence (the coherence cliff); MT-Bench / AlpacaEval (open-ended helpfulness retention).

The single most important taxonomy insight. Conditional steering (CAST and its descendants) is not a peer method — it is a meta-layer (axis 5). It wraps any axis-1–4 behavior injector and gates it on context, which is why it stacks orthogonally with almost everything. Stackability is precise: two methods stack iff they differ on axis 1 (site) OR are orthogonal on axis 2 (direction); they compete iff same site + same direction + different axis-4 operation (additive vs rotational on the same plane), or if they jointly blow the norm/manifold budget. Fixing axes 8–12 to the Euclidean/static/single-vector defaults is exactly the “naive additive steering” the Rogue Scalpel punishes.
