# Awesome LLM Steering

> A curated literature survey of activation / representation steering for large language models — the **literature backbone** of the [steeringresearch](./README.md) autoresearch project. Every paper is cited with a clickable arXiv link, organized by method family, oldest-to-newest within each section.
>
> **HTML version:** [docs/awesome-steering.html](./docs/awesome-steering.html) (GitHub-Pages page, same editorial style as the dashboard).

---

## How to navigate

- **Sections 1–7** trace the *method families*, from the train-free linear activation-addition lineage (§1) through conditional gating (§2), learned/distilled steering (§3), geometry-aware steering (§4), sparse/SAE-feature steering (§5), attention/KV/decoding-site steering (§6), and composition/stacking (§7).
- **Section 8** covers the safety frontier — why steering quietly breaks alignment (the *Rogue Scalpel*) and the scaling-law / refusal-circuit work around it.
- **Section 9** is the **evaluation substrate** — the benchmarks and datasets every steering experiment reports against.
- **Section 10** is the synthesizing artifact: the **12-axis intervention taxonomy**, a single table that locates every method in §1–§7 along WHERE / WHAT / HOW-MUCH / HOW / WHEN / WHICH-TOKENS / HOW-DERIVED plus the five geometry meta-axes (geometry, metric, identifiability, dynamics, basis).
- Each paper is a bullet: **Title** — Authors, YEAR VENUE. [arXiv:ID](https://arxiv.org/abs/ID). One-line method. Key result.
- A paper that *gates* other methods (CAST, Selective, FineSteer-SCS) is a **meta-layer**; a paper that *injects* behavior (CAA, FLAS) is an **injector**. The distinction drives §7 and §10.

**Provenance & caveat.** This survey is synthesized from the research transcripts in [`corpus/`](./corpus/) (SuperGrok Expert-mode literature sessions captured 2026-05-29, plus targeted arXiv sweeps). The corpus ran multiple ID-verification rounds, so **arXiv IDs are mostly real on independent spot-checks**, but the underlying transcripts are known to *fabricate* model-usage and quantitative details (e.g. an invented "Gemma-4-E4b-it"; newest open family is Gemma-3). Quantitative claims and "uses Gemma-X" assertions are tagged **[NEEDS VERIFICATION]** where the corpus flagged them, and arXiv IDs the corpus could not confirm are tagged **[UNVERIFIED]** with the title preserved for lookup. The *method-family taxonomy and stack-vs-compete logic* (derived from intervention site + mechanism) is the most trustworthy axis and is presented as the editorial spine.

**Paper count:** 60 entries across **10 sections**.

## 1. Foundations — the activation-addition lineage

The train-free, linear, residual-stream-addition family: compute a direction (difference-of-means or PCA on contrastive pairs) and add/subtract/project it at inference time, no weight updates.

- **Inference-Time Intervention: Eliciting Truthful Answers from a Language Model (ITI)** — Kenneth Li, Oam Patel, Fernanda Viégas, Hanspeter Pfister, Martin Wattenberg, 2023 NeurIPS (spotlight). [arXiv:2306.03341](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.03341). Shifts activations along truthful-vs-false directions on a small number of attention heads (difference-of-means on heads, found via linear probing). Key result: on Alpaca, improves truthfulness on TruthfulQA from 32.5% → 65.1%.
- **Steering Language Models With Activation Engineering (ActAdd)** — Alexander Matt Turner, Lisa Thiergart, Gavin Leech, David Udell, Juan J. Vazquez, Ulisse Mini, Monte MacDiarmid, 2023 (rev. 2024) arXiv. [arXiv:2308.10248](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.10248). Contrasts intermediate activations on prompt pairs ("Love" − "Hate") to compute a steering vector added during the forward pass. Key result: SOTA negative-to-positive sentiment shift and detoxification with single-pair data efficiency, preserving off-target capabilities.
- **Representation Engineering: A Top-Down Approach to AI Transparency (RepE)** — Andy Zou, Long Phan, Sarah Chen, James Campbell, Phillip Guo, … Dan Hendrycks (21 authors), 2023 arXiv. [arXiv:2310.01405](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.01405). Places population-level representations (not neurons/circuits) at the center; contrastive reading vectors monitor and manipulate high-level concepts. Key result: reading vectors achieve >90% accuracy distinguishing harmful/harmless instructions even under jailbreaks; emotion steering increases harmful compliance despite RLHF.
- **Steering Llama 2 via Contrastive Activation Addition (CAA)** — Nina Panickssery, Nick Gabrieli, Julian Schulz, Meg Tong, Evan Hubinger, Alexander Matt Turner, 2024 ACL. [arXiv:2312.06681](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.06681). Averages the residual-stream activation difference between positive/negative behavior example pairs; adds the vector at all post-prompt positions with a signed coefficient. Key result: significantly alters behavior (sycophancy, hallucination, refusal) on Llama-2, stacks on top of finetuning + system prompts, minimal capability loss.
- **Refusal in Language Models Is Mediated by a Single Direction** — Andy Arditi, Oscar Obeso, Aaquib Syed, Daniel Paleka, Nina Panickssery, Wes Gurnee, Neel Nanda, 2024 NeurIPS. [arXiv:2406.11717](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.11717). Difference-of-means of harmful vs harmless instruction activations yields a single refusal direction; ablate (project out) to bypass refusal, add to induce it. Key result: erasing the direction prevents refusal of harmful instructions while adding it elicits refusal on harmless ones, across 13 models up to 72B.
- **Steering Activation Interventions Can Be Transferred Between Large Language Models** — Narmeen Oozeer, Dhruv Nathawani, Nirmalendu Prakash, Michael Lan, Abir Harrasse, Amirali Abdullah, 2025 ICML (poster). [arXiv:2503.04429](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.04429). Transfers pre-computed difference-of-means steering vectors across distinct LLMs for the same safety behaviors. Key result: successful predictable transfer for both backdoor removal and harmful-prompt refusal.
- **Scaling Laws for Activation Steering with Llama 2 Models and Refusal Mechanisms** — Ali et al., 2025 arXiv. [arXiv:2507.11771](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.11771). A CAA scaling study across Llama-2 sizes. Key result: steering effectiveness grows with model size but saturates differently for refusal vs other behaviors. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **StyleVector: Personalized Text Generation with Contrastive Activation Steering** — J. Zhang, Wenjie Wang, Tat-Seng Chua et al., 2025 ACL (long). [UNVERIFIED — ACL Anthology confirmed, no standalone arXiv ID in sources; search "StyleVector contrastive activation"]. Derives a user-specific style vector by contrasting authentic-user vs generic-model activations, added for inference-time personalization. Key result: matches/exceeds RAG and PEFT on stylistic consistency at negligible latency/storage. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]

## 2. Conditional / gated steering (the meta-layer)

These are not peer injectors — they are a **meta-layer** that reads context and decides *whether* to apply a behavior vector. Because the gate is a read-only probe on a different axis/layer than the write, it stacks on top of nearly every method in §1.

- **Programming Refusal with Conditional Activation Steering (CAST)** — Bruce W. Lee, Inkit Padhi, Karthikeyan Natesan Ramamurthy, Erik Miehling, Pierre Dognin, Manish Nagireddy, Amit Dhurandhar, 2025 ICLR (Spotlight). [arXiv:2409.05907](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.05907). Introduces a *condition vector* (PCA on contrastive prompt activations) + thresholding (`sim(h, proj(h|c)) > θ`) that gates a behavior vector; supports OR/AND/complement logical composition. Key result: rules like "if input is about hate speech then refuse" keep harmful-refusal high while harmless-refusal stays ≈2% (Qwen-1.5-Chat-1.8B). [NEEDS VERIFICATION on quantitative gap]
- **FineSteer: A Unified Framework for Fine-Grained Inference-Time Steering in LLMs** — Zixuan Weng, Jinghuai Zhang, Kunlin Cai, Ying Li, Peiran Wang, Yuan Tian, 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2604.15488](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15488). Two-stage: Subspace-guided Conditional Steering (SCS), an energy-ratio gate that avoids unnecessary steering, then Mixture-of-Steering-Experts (MoSE) for multimodal behaviors. Key result: +7.6% on TruthfulQA over Llama-3, near-100% safety with minimal utility loss; SCS is a direct CAST successor. *Gemma-2-9B-IT.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Selective Steering: Norm-Preserving Control Through Discriminative Layer Selection** — Quy-Anh Dang, Chris Ngo, 2026 ACL. [arXiv:2601.19375](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19375). A norm-preserving rotation applied *only* at discriminative layers where class means have opposite sign (`µ_pos·µ_neg ≤ 0`) — a layer-gate. Key result: 5.5× higher attack success than priors, zero perplexity violations, ≈100% capability retention; on gemma-2-2b reaches 82.69% ASR where SAS=0% and AAS=74.04%. *Gemma-2-2B-IT / 9B-IT.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **In-Distribution Steering: Balancing Control and Coherence in Activation Interventions (IDS)** — (team reporting IDS; CAA/MERA comparisons), 2025 arXiv. [arXiv:2510.13285](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.13285). Input-dependent contextual modulation keeps steered activations inside the model's natural distribution before addition. Key result: average rank 1.67 across 6 behaviors vs CAA-1's 3.07; refusal SPI 0.92 on Gemma-2-2B at perplexity <8. *Gemma-2-2B-IT / 9B-IT.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Contextual Linear Activation Steering of Language Models** — Brandon Hsu et al., 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2604.24693](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24693). Extends linear difference-of-means addition with input-dependent contextual modulation (linear projection conditioned on the prompt embedding) before residual addition. Key result: stable multi-turn coherence gains over baseline CAA while preserving >98% MMLU. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]

## 3. Learned / flow / distilled steering

Methods that *learn* the intervention — a low-rank map, a token-specific gain field, a velocity field, or a hypernetwork — rather than reading a fixed difference-of-means vector.

- **ReFT / LoReFT: Representation Finetuning for Language Models** — Zhengxuan Wu, Aryaman Arora, Zheng Wang, Atticus Geiger, Dan Jurafsky, Christopher D. Manning, Christopher Potts, 2024 NeurIPS. [arXiv:2404.03592](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.03592). Learns low-rank interventions on frozen-model hidden representations (a learned subspace edit) instead of weight updates. Key result: 15×–65× more parameter-efficient than prior PEFT while competitive/superior on commonsense and reasoning suites.
- **BiPO / Improved Representation Steering for Language Models (RePS)** — Zhengxuan Wu et al., 2025 NeurIPS (poster). [arXiv:2505.20809](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.20809). A bidirectional preference objective (steer *and* suppress) trains low-rank reference-free preference steering. Key result: outperforms LM-objective training and narrows the gap to prompting on Gemma 2B–27B, resilient to jailbreaks, low parameter count. *Gemma-2-2B/9B, Gemma-3-12B/27B.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **HyperSteer: Hypernetwork-Generated Steering Vectors** — (HyperSteer team), 2025 arXiv. [arXiv:2506.03292](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.03292). A hypernetwork generates a steering vector on-the-fly conditioned on a natural-language prompt, replacing per-behavior contrast extraction. Key result: prompt-conditioned vectors generalize across behaviors without re-extraction. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Steer Like the LLM: Activation Steering that Mimics Prompting (PSR)** — Geert Heyman, Frederik Vandeputte, 2026 ICML. [arXiv:2605.03907](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03907). Formulates prompt steering as activation steering and distills the *token-specific* gain `g(h_t)` that real prompting induces — a learned per-token coefficient field on a fixed direction. Key result: PSR models outperform existing activation steering and compare favorably to prompting on AxBench + persona steering (held-out scores 0.618 at 2B-L20, 0.900 at 9B-L20). *Gemma-2-2B-IT / 9B-IT.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Beyond Steering Vector: Flow-based Activation Steering for Inference-Time Intervention (FLAS)** — Zehao Jin, Ruixuan Deng, Junran Wang, Xinjie Shen, Chao Zhang, 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2605.05892](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05892). Learns a concept-conditioned velocity field `v_t(h,t,c)` that transports unsteered activations to steered ones via multi-step integration (curved, token-varying trajectories) from a single ~97.6M-param FlowBlock. Key result: first learned method to consistently beat prompting on AxBench held-out (HMean 1.015 on Gemma-2-2B-IT, 1.113 on 9B-IT) without per-concept tuning. *Gemma-2-2B-IT / 9B-IT.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **YaPO: Learnable Sparse Activation Steering Vectors for Domain Adaptation** — Hadi Abdine, Preslav Nakov, Michalis Vazirgiannis, Guokan Shang et al., 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2601.08441](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.08441). Reference-free "Yet another Policy Optimization" learns trainable *sparse* steering vectors in a pretrained SAE latent space with a BiPO-style objective, added at inference. Key result: faster convergence, no measurable MMLU degradation, consistent gains over CAA on safety. *Gemma-2-2B (main), Gemma-2-9B-It (appendix).* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **LinEAS: End-to-end Learning of Activation Steering with a Distributional Loss** — (NeurIPS 2025 team), 2025 NeurIPS (poster). [UNVERIFIED — NeurIPS poster confirmed, no arXiv ID in sources; search "LinEAS activation steering"]. Trains linear maps with a global distributional loss across all layers plus sparsifying regularizers. Key result: beats comparable baselines on toxicity mitigation using unpaired data only, competitive with oracle methods. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Activation Steering for Aligned Open-ended Generation without Sacrificing Coherence (StTP / StMP)** — (StTP/StMP team), 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2604.08169](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08169). Targeted residual-stream addition with trait prompts (StTP) and multi-prompt (StMP) to restore honesty/compassion under malicious system prompts. Key result: recovers target traits while preserving MMLU / MT-Bench / AlpacaEval and producing less repetition. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]

## 4. Geometry-aware steering

The 2026 "geometry wave": the residual stream is not flat Euclidean ℝ^d. These works replace *find the right direction* with *find the right geometry* (curved paths, spherical/hyperbolic/cylindrical metrics, gauge/identifiability, trajectory dynamics).

- **Angular Steering** — (Angular Steering team), 2025 arXiv. [arXiv:2510.26243](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.26243). Rotates the activation within a 2D plane toward the target direction instead of adding (spherical-family anchor). Key result: directional control without increasing norm — but exhibits a norm-violation flaw on small (<7B) models that Selective Steering later fixes. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **GeoSteer: Faithful Chain-of-Thought Steering via Latent Manifold Gradients** — Kazama et al., 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2601.10229](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10229). Steers reasoning by following gradients *along* the latent manifold across CoT steps — trajectory-aware, manifold-gradient (axes 8 + 11). Key result: +0.9 accuracy / +4.5 reasoning-quality points avg vs baseline. [NEEDS VERIFICATION / NEEDS REPLICATION]
- **On the Non-Identifiability of Steering Vectors** — Venkatesh & Kurapath (Manipal), 2026 ICLR (workshop). [arXiv:2602.06801](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06801). A theory result: under white-box single-layer access, steering vectors are fundamentally non-identifiable — large equivalence classes of behaviorally indistinguishable interventions exist (the null space of downstream readouts is large). Key result: orthogonal perturbations achieve near-equivalent efficacy; behavioral testing alone cannot recover "the" direction — structural constraints are required (axis 10).
- **Curveball Steering: The Right Direction To Steer Isn't Always Linear** — Raval, Song, Wu, Harrasse, Phillips, Barez, Abdullah, 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2603.09313](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09313). The optimal steering path is curved; respecting manifold curvature beats a global linear direction (axis 8: path curvature). Key result: geometry-aware nonlinear steering reduces off-manifold displacement at matched behavior change. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Not All Latent Spaces Are Flat: Hyperbolic Concept Control (HyCon)** — Briglia, Facchiano, Cursi, Sampieri, Rodolà et al., 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2603.14093](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14093). Models hierarchical/tree-like concepts in *hyperbolic* (negative-curvature) space, where they embed with low distortion (axis 9: hyperbolic metric). Key result: increased stability ("geometric pacing under strong steering") vs Euclidean, where directions are only locally valid. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Global Evolutionary Refined Steering (GER-steer): Refining Activation Steering via Cross-Layer Consistency** — Xinyan Jiang, Wenjing Yu, Di Wang, Lijie Hu, 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2603.12298](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12298). Training-free framework extracting a global invariant direction across layers via spectral consensus + evolutionary refinement before residual addition. Key result: AdvBench refusal ↑ to 0.948 on Gemma-2-9B-it (vs ~0.77 baselines), superior transferability across five domains. *Gemma-2-9B-it.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **The Cylindrical Representation Hypothesis (CRH)** — Gao, Zhang, Liu, … Chen (MBZUAI), 2026 ICML. [arXiv:2605.01844](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01844). Representation differences live on a *cylinder* S^{d-1}×ℝ: a radial (magnitude) component × an angular (semantic direction) component (axis 9). Key result: decomposing steering into radius (strength) × angle (meaning) makes it more stable and predictable, explaining why plain additive coupling is unstable. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Manifold Steering** — Wurgaft, Rager, Kowal, … Goodman, Fel, Geiger, Lubana (Goodfire / Stanford / Harvard), 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2605.05115](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05115). Fits an activation manifold M_h and a behavior manifold M_y; steering *along* M_h yields trajectories that follow M_y, while linear Euclidean steering cuts through off-manifold regions. Key result: "recasts the core problem of steering from finding the right direction to finding the right geometry"; bidirectional M_h↔M_y link holds across reasoning geometries and a video world model. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Manifold-Guided Attention Steering (MAGS)** — Ian Li, Kapilesh Guruprasad, Raunak Sengupta, Ninad Satish, Loris D'Antoni, Rose Yu, 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2605.21770](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21770). Learns a low-dimensional "correctness manifold" vs error subspace from contrastive traces, then conditionally projects attention-head outputs back onto the manifold before W_O, writing the corrected vector into the KV cache (axes 1 + 11). Key result: up to +10.8% accuracy across MATH-500/GSM8K/HumanEval/MBPP at negligible overhead, beating ITI / Angular / Contrastive Decoding. *Corpus reports "Gemma-4-E4b-it" — that family does not exist; treat the Gemma claim as fabricated.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Steered LLM Activations are Non-Surjective** — Aayush Mishra, Daniel Khashabi, Anqi Liu, 2026 arXiv (v2). [arXiv:2604.09839](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09839). Frames activation addition as a surjectivity problem and proves steered states lie off the manifold reachable by any discrete prompt sequence. Key result: "almost surely no prompt can reproduce the same internal behavior induced by steering"; empirical L2 distances confirm no close natural preimages on Gemma-3-1B, Llama-3.2-1B, Qwen-2.5-0.5B. *Gemma-3-1B.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]

## 5. Sparse / SAE-feature steering

Steering in the *sparse, interpretable* basis of a Sparse Autoencoder — clamp or add along monosemantic feature directions rather than dense residual directions. Anchored by the GemmaScope SAE suite.

- **Towards Monosemanticity: Decomposing Language Models With Dictionary Learning** — Trenton Bricken, Adly Templeton, … Chris Olah (Anthropic), 2023 Transformer Circuits. [transformer-circuits.pub/2023/monosemantic-features](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2023/monosemantic-features/index.html) (no arXiv). First large-scale demonstration that SAEs recover thousands of monosemantic, causally steerable features. Key result: a 512-neuron MLP yields >4,096 (up to 131k) interpretable features; feature pinning steers output (base64, Arabic script).
- **Jumping Ahead: Improving Reconstruction Fidelity with JumpReLU Sparse Autoencoders** — Senthooran Rajamanoharan, Tom Lieberum, Nicolas Sonnerat, Arthur Conmy, Vikrant Varma, János Kramár, Neel Nanda (DeepMind), 2024 arXiv. [arXiv:2407.14435](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.14435). Replaces ReLU with a learned-threshold JumpReLU + direct L0 penalty via straight-through estimators. Key result: SOTA reconstruction-sparsity Pareto frontier on Gemma-2-9B residual activations (beats Gated and TopK SAEs). *Gemma-2-9B.*
- **Scaling Monosemanticity: Extracting Interpretable Features from Claude 3 Sonnet (Golden Gate Claude)** — Adly Templeton, Tom Conerly, … Tom Henighan (Anthropic), 2024 Transformer Circuits. [transformer-circuits.pub/2024/scaling-monosemanticity](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2024/scaling-monosemanticity/) (no arXiv). Scales SAEs to production Claude 3 Sonnet; feature clamping as a steering primitive. Key result: dictionaries up to ~34M features; the Golden Gate Bridge feature clamped to 10× max makes the model obsess about the bridge while staying coherent.
- **Gemma Scope: Open Sparse Autoencoders Everywhere All At Once on Gemma 2** — Tom Lieberum, Senthooran Rajamanoharan, Arthur Conmy, Lewis Smith, Nicolas Sonnerat, Vikrant Varma, János Kramár, Anca Dragan, Rohin Shah, Neel Nanda (DeepMind), 2024 BlackboxNLP. [arXiv:2408.05147](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147). Open release of JumpReLU SAEs covering every layer/sublayer of Gemma 2. Key result: >400 SAEs / >30M features; the infrastructure underpinning nearly all small-Gemma sparse steering. *Gemma-2-2B / 9B (select 27B).*
- **Improving Steering Vectors by Targeting Sparse Autoencoder Features (SAE-TS)** — Sviatoslav Chalnev, Matthew Siu, Arthur Conmy, 2024 (rev. 2025) arXiv. [arXiv:2411.02193](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.02193). Uses SAEs to causally measure side-effects of any steering vector, then optimizes a replacement that activates only the desired feature while suppressing others. Key result: better effect/coherence trade-off than CAA and single-SAE-feature steering. *Gemma-2-2B (layer-12 SAE).*
- **Interpretable Steering of Large Language Models with Feature Guided Activation Additions (FGAA)** — Samuel Soo, Chen Guang, Wesley Teng, Chandrasekaran Balaganesh, Tan Guoxian, Yan Ming, 2025 arXiv. [arXiv:2501.09929](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.09929). Combines CAA + SAE-TS in the SAE latent space (16,384 features, Gemma Scope residual layer 12), optimizing feature selection. Key result: outperforms CAA, SAE-decoder steering, and SAE-TS; optimal in 8/9 tasks on 2B. *Gemma-2-2B / 9B.*
- **AxBench: Steering LLMs? Even Simple Baselines Outperform Sparse Autoencoders** — Zhengxuan Wu, Aryaman Arora, Atticus Geiger, Zheng Wang, Jing Huang, Dan Jurafsky, Christopher D. Manning, Christopher Potts (Stanford NLP), 2025 ICML (spotlight). [arXiv:2501.17148](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.17148). Apples-to-apples benchmark separating concept detection vs steering across prompting / finetuning / probes / steering vectors / ReFT / SAEs. Key result (verbatim): *"On both evaluations, SAEs are not competitive"* — prompting wins steering, linear probes win detection; ReFT-r1 is the competitive interpretable method. *Gemma-2-2B / 9B.* (Cross-listed in §9.)
- **Steering Large Language Model Activations in Sparse Spaces (SAS)** — Reza Bayat, Ali Rahimi-Kalahroudi, Mohammad Pezeshki, Sarath Chandar, Pascal Vincent, 2025 arXiv. [arXiv:2503.00177](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.00177). Isolates behavior-specific SAE features via contrastive prompt-pairing to reinforce/suppress behaviors in sparse space. Key result: nuanced behavioral modulation at λ=±1, τ=0.7; scaling SAEs improves monosemanticity, outperforming dense baselines. *Gemma-2-2B (main), 9B (appendix).*
- **Interpretable LLM Guardrails via Sparse Representation Steering (SRS)** — He et al., 2025 arXiv. [arXiv:2503.16851](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.16851). Disentangles activations via Gemma-Scope SAE into monosemantic features, selecting dimensions via bidirectional KL on positive/negative pairs. Key result: improved controllability across safety/fairness/truthfulness (single- and multi-attribute) while preserving linguistic quality. *Gemma-2-2B-IT / 9B-IT.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **CorrSteer: Generation-Time LLM Steering via Correlated Sparse Autoencoder Feature Selection** — Seonglae Cho, Zekun Wu, Adriano Koshiyama, 2025 (v3 2026) arXiv. [arXiv:2508.12535](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.12535). Correlates SAE-feature activations on generated tokens with correctness/safety labels on-the-fly (O(1) streaming, zero contrast dataset), then steers with top-correlated features. Key result: +3.3% MMLU and +27.1% HarmBench on Gemma-2-2B with lower side-effect ratio than finetuning. *Gemma-2-2B (primary), Llama-3.1-8B.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Steered Generation via Gradient-Based Optimization on Sparse Query Features (Prototype-Based Sparse Steering)** — Sumanta Bhattacharyya et al., 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2605.23040](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23040). Applies SAEs to query activations, decomposes into sparse features, then gradient-optimizes during inference to align with class prototypes before residual addition. Key result: progressive refinement with qualitative + cognitive-style accuracy gains over dense query steering (Bloom/Phi). [NEEDS VERIFICATION]

## 6. Attention / KV / decoding-site steering

Interventions that leave the residual stream and act on the *attention mechanism* (Q/V vectors, attention scores), the *KV cache*, or the *decoding distribution* — orthogonal sites that stack with residual methods and often cost less.

- **DoLa: Decoding by Contrasting Layers Improves Factuality in Large Language Models** — Yung-Sung Chuang et al., 2023 ICLR. [arXiv:2309.03883](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.03883). Contrasts logits from a mature vs premature layer at decode time to surface factual knowledge (logit-site, post all residual edits). Key result: improves TruthfulQA and factual generation across LLaMA sizes without retraining.
- **Tell Your Model Where to Attend: Post-hoc Attention STeering Approach (PASTA)** — Qingru Zhang, Chandan Singh, Liyuan Liu, Xiaodong Liu, Bin Yu, Jianfeng Gao, Tuo Zhao, 2024 ICLR. [arXiv:2311.02262](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.02262). Profiles a small set of attention heads offline, then upweights user-marked tokens (and downweights others) in those heads at inference. Key result: +22% average accuracy on LLaMA-7B across instruction-following / knowledge-integration tasks.
- **Spotlight Your Instructions: Instruction-following with Dynamic Attention Steering (SpotLight)** — Praveen Venkateswaran, Danish Contractor, 2025 EACL (long). [arXiv:2505.12025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.12025). Monitors natural attention mass on instruction tokens each step and applies a proportional, only-when-needed logit bias to attention scores (dynamic, latency-aware). Key result: fixes PASTA's offline-profiling cost and rigid targeting; strong on multi-turn / refusal / complex instructions.
- **KV Cache Steering for Controlling Frozen LLMs** — Max Belitsky, Dawid J. Kopiczko, Michael Dorkenwald, M. Jehanzeb Mirza, James R. Glass, Cees G. M. Snoek, Yuki M. Asano, 2025 arXiv (v2). [arXiv:2507.08799](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.08799). Builds steering vectors from teacher traces and applies a single additive edit directly to the KV cache after prompt processing (zero per-token overhead). Key result: +7.4% MATH, +4.6% GPQA-Diamond on Llama-3.1-70B; 95% style-transfer success on small models. [NEEDS VERIFICATION — no Gemma in paper]
- **DISCO: Disentangled Communication Steering for Large Language Models** — Max Torop, Aria Masoomi, Masih Eskandar, Jennifer Dy, 2025 NeurIPS. [arXiv:2509.16820](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.16820). Injects steering vectors directly into the query and value spaces inside attention heads (not the residual stream) for disentangled control. Key result: steering efficacy up to 19.1% higher than the runner-up baseline. *Gemma-2-9B.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Memory Inception: Latent-Space KV Cache Manipulation for Steering LLMs** — Andy Zeyi Liu, Michael Zhang, Ilana Greenberg, Adam Alnasser, Lucas Baker, John Sous, 2026 arXiv (v2). [arXiv:2605.06225](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06225). Training-free "memory-bank" encoding of reminder text into frozen-model KV vectors injected at selected layers as hidden slots. Key result: 60–118× KV-storage reduction vs full prompting; beats CAA on control-drift and prompting on structured reasoning. [NEEDS VERIFICATION — no Gemma tested]
- **Periodic KV Cache Consolidation for Generalised Reasoning** — A. Oomerjee et al., 2025 arXiv (v3). [arXiv:2505.16950](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.16950). A non-causal auxiliary "Cache Processor" rewrites KV entries at reasoning-step boundaries, consolidating recent + top-k prior entries. Key result: better long-horizon generalization; frames KV edits as a third pillar alongside token-mediated and residual steering. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Don't Lose Focus: Activation Steering via Key-Orthogonal Projection (SKOP)** — (SKOP / Prompt-Activation Duality team), 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2605.06342](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06342). Projects steering vectors orthogonal to the key matrix so additions don't contaminate the KV cache while preserving addition mechanics. Key result: retains >95% of vanilla query-space steering efficacy while keeping utility degradation under 10%; best steering–utility trade-off (rank 2/44) on Gemma-2-9B-IT. *Gemma-2-9B-IT.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Improving Activation Steering via Attention-Level Delta Gating and Cropping (GCAD)** — (GCAD team), 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2605.10664 — UNVERIFIED; title + method confirmed from abstracts, ID flagged in source]. Cropped, gated multi-layer attention-delta steering (instead of raw residual) to mitigate KV-cache contamination. Key result: higher per-trait expression Δ and coherence retention than single-layer residual baselines on Llama-3.1-8B / Qwen2.5-7B. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **AntiPaSTO: Self-Supervised Honesty Steering via Anti-Parallel Representations** — Michael J. Clark et al., 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2601.07473](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.07473). Learns antiparallel steering directions operating through attention and KV-cache pathways (beyond residual) for bidirectional control. Key result: 6.9× Steering F1 over prompting on Gemma-3-1B (DailyDilemmas); tables span Gemma-3-270M → 12B. *Gemma-3-270M / 1B / 4B / 12B.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]

## 7. Composition / stacking

Algebras and frameworks built to combine multiple steering goals *without interference* — the explicit answer to "can I run several safety vectors at once?"

- **Steering Large Language Models using Conceptors: Improving Addition-Based Activation Engineering** — Joris Postmus, Steven Abreu, Alexander Müller et al., 2024 arXiv. [arXiv:2410.16314](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.16314). Represents sets of activation vectors as ellipsoidal "conceptor" / steering-matrix regions, enabling AND/OR/NOT Boolean composition of multiple goals. Key result: the AND-combined conceptor outperformed mean-combined steering vectors on multi-goal tasks. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **From Steering Vectors to Conceptors: Compositional Affine Activation Steering for LLMs** — Abreu et al., 2025 OpenReview. (conceptor follow-on, affine composition). Conceptor matrices for affine composition of addition vectors. Key result: provably optimal compositional control with low utility loss on multi-property steering. [UNVERIFIED — OpenReview, no arXiv ID in sources] [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **EasyEdit2: An Easy-to-use Steering Framework for Editing LLMs** — (EasyEdit2 team), 2025 arXiv. [arXiv:2504.15133](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.15133). Unified framework implementing CAA/STA + vector merging (Linear / TIES / DARE-TIES) for multi-task residual addition in one intervention. Key result: merged safety+sentiment CAA vectors reach joint scores (e.g. 66.89 safety on Gemma) vs single-task baselines. *Gemma (layer-24), Qwen.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Persona Vectors: Monitoring and Controlling Character Traits in Language Models** — (Anthropic persona-vectors team), 2025 arXiv. [arXiv:2507.21509](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.21509). Defines separate, reusable directions for distinct traits (evil, sycophancy, hallucination) automatically from natural-language trait descriptions — a *bank* of vectors to monitor/apply per property. Key result: persona vectors predict and preventatively steer trait shifts (including during finetuning). [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Fine-Grained Activation Steering: Steering Less, Achieving More (AUSteer)** — Zijian Feng, Tianjiao Li, Zixiao Zhu, Hanzhang Zhou, Junlang Qian, Li Zhang, Jia Jim Deryl Chua, Lee Onn Mak, Gee Wah Ng, Kezhi Mao, 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2602.04428](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04428). Identifies discriminative atomic units (AUs) via activation momenta on contrastive samples and assigns adaptive per-unit strengths, steering only beneficial activations. Key result: +1.85 over SADI on LLaMA2-7B-Chat, +1.91 on Gemma2-9B-it, steering ≤100 activations vs thousands for CAA/STA. *Gemma2-9B-it.* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]

## 8. Safety & the Rogue Scalpel

The frontier finding that steering damages alignment as a side effect of leaving the manifold — plus the mechanistic and scaling work that frames it.

- **The Rogue Scalpel: Activation Steering Compromises LLM Safety** — Anton Korznikov, Andrey Galichin, Alexey Dontsov, Alexey Rogov, Ivan Oseledets, Elena Tutubalina, 2025 (v2 2026) ICML. [arXiv:2509.22067](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.22067). On JailbreakBench (baseline compliance 0%), shows random/benign steering breaks refusal. Key results: random directions raise compliance to 1–13%; benign SAE features are as bad or worse (817/1000 jailbreak ≥1 prompt); damage peaks in early-middle layers; averaging just 20 weak vectors yields a universal attack (Falcon3-7B 5.7%→63.4%); Appendix E — attacks are near-orthogonal to the refusal direction (mean cos 0.027±0.021), so the mechanism is off-manifold displacement, not refusal cancellation.
- **What Drives Representation Steering? A Mechanistic Case Study on Steering Refusal** — Stephen Cheng, Sarah Wiegreffe, Dinesh Manocha, 2026 arXiv. [arXiv:2604.08524](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08524). Circuit analysis of refusal steering vectors; primary interaction is via the OV circuit (attention output), largely ignoring QK; includes 90–99% sparsification. Key result: freezing all attention scores during steering drops performance by only 8.75%; vectors sparsify to 90–99% with most performance retained. *Gemma-2-2B (layer-15).* [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Scaling Laws for Activation Steering with Llama 2 Models and Refusal Mechanisms** — Ali et al., 2025 arXiv. [arXiv:2507.11771](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.11771). Explicit scaling laws for CAA-style refusal steering. Key result: effectiveness grows with model size but saturates differently for refusal. (Cross-listed from §1.) [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- **Refusal Steering: Fine-grained Control over LLM Refusal Behaviour for Sensitive Topics** — Iker García-Ferrero, David Montero, Roman Orus, 2025 arXiv. [arXiv:2512.16602](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.16602). Replaces pattern-based refusal detection with an LLM-as-judge and a ridge-regularized refusal–compliance direction to remove political refusal while retaining harmful-content safety. Key result: maintains safety on JailbreakBench and near-baseline general performance. [NEEDS VERIFICATION]

## 9. Evaluation & benchmarks

The measurement substrate. Steering has four things to measure at once — did behavior change, did capability survive, did text stay coherent, did safety survive — plus selectivity for gated methods.

- **AxBench: Steering LLMs? Even Simple Baselines Outperform Sparse Autoencoders** — Wu, Arora, Geiger, Wang, Huang, Jurafsky, Manning, Potts (Stanford NLP), 2025 ICML (spotlight). [arXiv:2501.17148](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.17148). The canonical fine-grained concept-steering benchmark and apples-to-apples harness (prompting vs probes vs SAE vs steering vs ReFT); supplies the 500-concept holdout used by FLAS/PSR. Key result: "SAEs are not competitive" for steering; prompting wins. *Gemma-2-2B / 9B.*
- **JailbreakBench** — the safety-integrity benchmark (100 harmful prompts, 10 categories) used as the Rogue Scalpel testbed; baseline compliance must be 0%, measure Compliance Rate under every steering condition. Mandatory for stacking/guard experiments. *Community benchmark; confirm split at run time.*
- **HarmBench** — broader harmful-behavior evaluation; cross-checks Compliance-Rate generalization beyond JailbreakBench. *Community benchmark.*
- **AdvBench** — harmful behaviors + strings; the refusal-rate target reported by GER-steer and Selective Steering. *Community benchmark.*
- **StrongREJECT** — stricter jailbreak scoring to avoid LLM-judge over-counting of "successful" jailbreaks. *Community benchmark.*
- **XSTest** — the over-refusal dual: benign prompts that *look* harmful, measuring the over-refusal cost of safety steering. *Community benchmark.*
- **TruthfulQA** — the truthfulness benchmark (MC + generative); ITI's home benchmark and the FineSteer +7.6% target. *Community benchmark.*
- **Capability & coherence probes** — MMLU / ARC / GSM8K / HellaSwag (capability-tax, ≤1–2% drop = acceptable), WikiText-103 perplexity + repetition/MAUVE + LLM-judge coherence (the coherence cliff), MT-Bench / AlpacaEval (open-ended helpfulness retention). *Standard community benchmarks; the corpus's measurement-axes substrate.*

## 10. The 12-axis intervention taxonomy

The synthesizing artifact. Any steering *method* is one setting along each of these axes; most "new methods" in the arXiv firehose are a new *point* in this space, not a new dimension. Axes 1–7 are the original knobs; axes 8–12 are **meta-axes** that change the *space* the first seven live in (the Euclidean/static/single-vector defaults of axes 8–12 are exactly the "naive additive steering" that the Rogue Scalpel punishes).

| # | Axis | Question | Default | Representative papers |
|---|------|----------|---------|-----------------------|
| 1 | **WHERE** (site) | which layer / stream / head | one mid layer | CAA §1, DISCO §6 (Q/V), PASTA/SpotLight §6 (attn scores), KV-Cache/MAGS §6, DoLa §6 (logits), ReFT §3 (low-rank weights) |
| 2 | **WHAT** (direction) | which vector / subspace | one fixed vector | refusal-direction §1, ReFT §3 (low-rank), SAE feature §5, conceptor ellipsoid §7, persona vector §7 |
| 3 | **HOW MUCH** (coeff) | edit magnitude | flat global α | global α (CAA §1); adaptive per-unit (AUSteer §7); token-specific g(h_t) (PSR §3); radius axis of CRH §4 |
| 4 | **HOW** (operation) | add / rotate / clamp / project / flow | add | add (CAA §1); rotate (Angular/Spherical/Selective §2,§4); project-out (refusal ablation §1, SKOP §6); flow/transport (FLAS §3) |
| 5 | **WHEN** (condition) | always, or gated on input | always | CAST §2; FineSteer-SCS energy-ratio gate §2; Selective discriminative-layer gate §2; persona preventative §7 |
| 6 | **WHICH TOKENS** (span) | all positions, or selected | all tokens | classic all-token (CAA §1); token-selective g(h_t) (PSR §3) |
| 7 | **HOW DERIVED** (source) | source of the vector | diff-of-means | diff-of-means / PCA (§1); SAE decoder column (§5); hypernetwork (HyperSteer §3); distilled/learned (PSR, FLAS, BiPO §3) |
| 8 | **GEOMETRY** (path) | chord vs geodesic | chord (linear) | Manifold §4, Curveball §4, GeoSteer §4 |
| 9 | **METRIC** (space) | which geometry | Euclidean | Spherical/Angular §4, HyCon §4 (hyperbolic), CRH §4 (cylindrical) |
| 10 | **IDENTIFIABILITY** (gauge) | which equivalent rep + null space | ignore it | Non-Identifiability §4 (optimize over the equivalence class) |
| 11 | **DYNAMICS** (time) | one-shot vs trajectory | one-shot | GeoSteer §4, MAGS §4/§6, FLAS §3 (multi-step flow) |
| 12 | **BASIS / SUPERPOSITION** | dense vs sparse + interference budget | dense, no budget | GemmaScope §5, FGAA §5, SAS §5, SAE-TS §5, CorrSteer §5 |

**The single most important taxonomy insight (from the corpus):** conditional steering (CAST and its descendants) is *not a peer method — it is a meta-layer* (axis 5). It wraps any axis-1–4 behavior injector and gates it on context, which is why it stacks orthogonally with almost everything. Stackability is precise: two methods **stack** iff they differ on axis 1 (site) OR are orthogonal on axis 2 (direction); they **compete** iff same site + same direction + different axis-4 operation (additive vs rotational on the same plane), or if they jointly blow the norm/manifold budget. See [`corpus/steering-stackable-vs-competing-analysis.md`](./corpus/steering-stackable-vs-competing-analysis.md) for the full pairwise decision matrix.
